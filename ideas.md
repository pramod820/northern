## Idea 1: "Rugged Naturalism" — Organic Industrial

**Design Movement:** Organic Brutalism meets Australian bush aesthetic — raw, earthy, and unapologetically grounded.

**Core Principles:**
1. Textural authenticity — rough-hewn surfaces, bark-like textures, grain overlays
2. Asymmetric weight — heavy typographic blocks counterbalanced by open space
3. Earth-first palette — no synthetic greens; colours drawn from eucalyptus bark, red soil, and dry grass
4. Functional honesty — every element serves a purpose, no decorative fluff

**Color Philosophy:**
- Primary: Deep charcoal (#1C1C1C) — the colour of burnt wood, grounding and authoritative
- Accent: Warm amber (#C4842D) — like afternoon sun through gum trees
- Secondary: Dusty sage (#7A8B6F) — subtle, natural green that doesn't scream "tree company"
- Background: Warm off-white (#F5F0EB) — parchment-like warmth
- Emotional intent: Trust through rawness, competence through restraint

**Layout Paradigm:** Stacked editorial blocks with full-bleed imagery. Content flows vertically in large, breathing sections. Left-aligned text with dramatic right-side imagery. No centred hero — instead, a split-screen approach with text on one side and a towering tree image on the other.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Bark texture overlay on section dividers — a subtle grain pattern
2. "Chainsaw cut" diagonal section transitions — angled clip-paths that feel like a saw cut through wood
3. Oversized service numbers (01, 02, 03...) in a stencil font behind service cards

**Interaction Philosophy:** Deliberate and weighty — elements slide in with purpose, not bounce. Scroll-triggered reveals feel like turning pages of a field manual.

**Animation:** Slow parallax on hero imagery. Service cards rise from below with a slight rotation. Numbers count up when "Why Choose Us" enters viewport. No playful bounces — everything moves with the gravity of a falling tree.

**Typography System:**
- Display: "DM Serif Display" — authoritative, editorial serif for headings
- Body: "Work Sans" — clean geometric sans that pairs well with the serif
- Accent: Monospace numerals for statistics and phone numbers

## Idea 2: "Precision Arboriculture" — Swiss-Inspired Technical Elegance

**Design Movement:** Swiss International Style adapted for trade services — clinical precision meets outdoor craft.

**Core Principles:**
1. Grid discipline — strict 12-column grid with mathematical spacing
2. Information hierarchy — data-driven layout where numbers and facts lead
3. Contrast through restraint — mostly monochrome with a single accent colour
4. Professional credibility — the site should feel like a corporate consultancy, not a local tradie

**Color Philosophy:**
- Primary: Forest black (#0D1B0E) — deep, almost-black green
- Accent: Safety orange (#E8651A) — the colour of chainsaw guards and hi-vis, signals action and urgency
- Neutral: Cool grey (#E8E8E8) — clinical, clean backgrounds
- White: Pure (#FFFFFF) — for maximum contrast
- Emotional intent: Precision, safety-consciousness, technical mastery

**Layout Paradigm:** Strict grid with modular cards. Content organised in a magazine-style layout with clear visual hierarchy. Large hero with overlaid statistics. Services displayed in a tight 3×3 grid. Sidebar navigation for service areas.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Technical line drawings of trees as decorative elements — like botanical illustrations
2. A persistent "emergency hotline" bar that follows scroll with pulsing orange indicator
3. Grid lines visible as faint background pattern, reinforcing precision

**Interaction Philosophy:** Snappy and responsive — hover states reveal additional information. Cards flip or expand to show service details. Everything responds instantly, like well-maintained equipment.

**Animation:** Crisp fade-ins with no easing overshoot. Grid elements stagger in with 50ms delays. The emergency bar pulses subtly. Scroll progress indicator along the side.

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Space Grotesk" — geometric, technical, modern
- Body: "IBM Plex Sans" — designed for readability, technical heritage
- Numbers: Tabular figures throughout for aligned statistics

## Idea 3: "Canopy & Craft" — Premium Artisan Service

**Design Movement:** Modern luxury meets Australian landscape photography — think premium property developer aesthetic applied to tree services.

**Core Principles:**
1. Cinematic imagery — full-bleed photography as the primary design element
2. Whisper-quiet typography — elegant, understated text that lets images speak
3. Generous negative space — premium brands breathe; this site should too
4. Storytelling flow — the page reads like a narrative, not a brochure

**Color Philosophy:**
- Primary: Deep evergreen (#1B3A2D) — rich, sophisticated forest green
- Accent: Warm gold (#B8963E) — subtle luxury without being gaudy
- Text: Soft charcoal (#2D2D2D) — easier on eyes than pure black
- Background: Warm cream (#FAFAF5) — barely-there warmth
- Dark sections: Rich forest (#0F2318) — for dramatic contrast sections
- Emotional intent: "These aren't just tree loppers — they're craftsmen who care about your property"

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-width cinematic sections alternating between light and dark backgrounds. Horizontal scroll gallery for portfolio. Services presented as large editorial cards with hover-reveal descriptions. Contact section as a dramatic dark-background finale.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Subtle leaf/branch SVG motifs that frame section edges — not cartoonish, more like pressed botanical specimens
2. A "ring of a tree trunk" cross-section graphic used as a section divider or loading element
3. Parallax depth on hero with layered foreground branches

**Interaction Philosophy:** Smooth and considered — like walking through a well-maintained garden. Hover states are gentle opacity shifts. Scroll animations are graceful, not aggressive.

**Animation:** Smooth scroll-linked parallax on hero. Sections fade up with 600ms ease. Gallery images have a subtle Ken Burns effect on hover. Service cards have a gentle lift with shadow deepening. The overall feeling is cinematic and calm.

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Playfair Display" — elegant serif with character, for major headings only
- Body: "Source Sans 3" — highly readable, professional sans-serif
- Accent: "Playfair Display" italic for quotes and testimonials
